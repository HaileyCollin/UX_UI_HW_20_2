NAMI Responsive Webpage Redesign

Our goal in this project was to give the NAMI Minnesota webpage a much needed tune up. The current look is outdated, unaligned, and overall hard to read .NAMI is a national grassroots organization dedicated to aiding the lives of individuals who personally suffer from mental illness or love someone who does. In this project, we focused on making the Minnesota branch’s website more visually appealing and giving it a more polished look.

Project Overview

The Idea

We like the thought behind NAMI and don’t want to take away from its core values. We wanted to make the website more appealing and easier to read.

Challenges

Challenges we faced were getting in contact with a stakeholder, issues with a huge yet important navigation, and getting ahold of people to interview for the project itself.

Methodology

We began by picking up the current website and looking at its other branches in different states, from this, we created a competitor analysis and began creating an interview plan. After interviewing and survey completed, we would move into ideation and from there wireframing.

When I made the user persona I knew I had the opportunity to make someone very relatable to almost anyone based off the answers we got from the interviews and after creating the empathy map. I used all this information and Corey Lynch was created.

Mood Boards

After all diagramming and mapping tactics were complete we were able to move into more visual design elements. I began working on mood boards while other group members worked on out style guide, user path, navigation components, and wireframes.

The Problem

Users on the NAMI MN webpage can’t find resources easily and must look through multiple pages to find the resources they are looking for. Users who are trying to find help for themselves or a loved one in a mental crisis are unable to quickly find what they need thus stressing them out further. As a result, users are deterred from using the NAMI MN webpage altogether. How might we make the critical content on the NAMI MN webpage findable?

The Solution

The solution was simple, we needed to take the things we liked from other similar websites we visited and build our own version of it with our own unique style. We didn’t need to change the content of NAMI, just the structure and style of it.

Testing & Iterating

User Testing

We tested the Lo-Fi prototype out and people seemed to like the general idea but there was a lot that left users wondering. For example, the layout was confusing without any images or colors and some of the text seemed really jammed in together. Once we got the Hi-Fi done users seemed to enjoy that one a lot. The colors stood out and were alert but not overwhelming and the text seemed easy to read. The new design overall gave people a more user-friendly feel and there weren’t any issues when looking for different resources.

Iterations

Iterations made for this project were small things like text size, some color choices that were in different areas to make certain text more visible, and the size of images/the image carousel. These small changes helped make the overall design that much more appealing.
